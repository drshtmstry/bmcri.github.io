About GNP Charitable Trust

Banas Dairy (A member of Amul, The Taste of India brand owner GCMMF Ltd.) floated an idea to establish a new trust to offer community services which was endorsed in its general body meeting organised on 7/7/2016. As an effect a new trust named Galbabhai Nanjibhai Patel Charitable trust was registered by the Charity Commissioner under certificate No. E/1530 Banaskantha on 17th day of September 2016.

Warning

A recent Advisory published by the National Medical Commission (NMC) of India has alerted students about the unscrupulous presence of elements misleading them by coming up with alluring admission offers of MBBS course admissions in medical colleges without attending the counselling sessions.

Statutory Warning

This is Government self-finance Institute (PPP Model). Admission (SFI & NRI) is through Central Admission Committee ( www.medadmgujarat.org ) and by merits only. There is no management quota. Even after last counseling, if seat is vacant, admission is not given by this institute. If any person demands money for admission to this institute, inform the Vigilance Cell of Government of Gujarat.

Initial Activities of GNP Charitable Trust

Free Checkups

Totally free of cost consultation, treatment and medicines to every patients. Total 3650 patients have been treated during the period of May-2016 to March-2017.

Blood Donation Camp

Blood Donation Camp was organized on 15/02/2017 as a part of Centurial birth celebration year of late Shri Galbabhai Nanjibhai Patel.

Banas Patrika

The monthly magazine Banas Patrika published by Banas Dairy covers human health related topics on Snake bite, Malaria, Diabetes etc. towards community health.

Denture Camp

Free Denture camps were organized at Taluka level during 05/03/2017 to 29/03/2017 under the National Oral Health Program. About 2873 patients took benefits.

MAA Card

Rural awareness about MAA CARD is busted. Total 14,599 MAA Cards were issued in the special camps organized by the organization.

Rural Health Camp

Rural Health Camps were organized at different villages to create awareness among people about different diseases due to contaminated water, cleanliness etc.
